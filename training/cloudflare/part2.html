2.1 DNS: The Internet's Address Book

DNS (Domain Name System) is the internet's address book — it translates human-friendly domain names (example.com) into the IP addresses computers actually use to find servers — and it's the foundation of nearly everything on Cloudflare (and the web). When you put a site on Cloudflare, you manage its DNS there, and DNS is what makes Cloudflare's proxying work (it points your domain at Cloudflare's network so Cloudflare can sit in front). Understanding DNS well is therefore essential, and it's a genuinely valuable skill on its own (DNS issues are behind a huge share of 'the website is down' problems). The key concepts: DNS records (the entries that say where things point — an A record points a domain to an IP, a CNAME points it to another domain, MX records route email, TXT records hold verification/config), the idea of propagation (DNS changes take time to spread), and, on Cloudflare specifically, the crucial proxy toggle (the orange cloud) that determines whether traffic goes through Cloudflare (proxied — getting all its benefits) or straight to your origin (DNS-only). Mastering DNS — the records, how to manage them, and Cloudflare's proxy concept — is the foundational skill that everything else builds on.

2.2 Managing DNS on Cloudflare (Practical)

DNS ON CLOUDFLARE
  COMMON DNS RECORD TYPES:
    · A      -> points a name to an IPv4 address (example.com -> 192.0.2.1)
    · AAAA   -> points to an IPv6 address
    · CNAME  -> points a name to ANOTHER name (www -> example.com)
    · MX     -> mail servers (routes email for the domain)
    · TXT    -> text records (domain verification, SPF/DKIM email auth, etc.)
    · NS     -> nameservers (Cloudflare gives you these when you add a site)
  ADDING A SITE TO CLOUDFLARE:
    1. add your domain in the dashboard 2. Cloudflare scans your existing records
    3. it gives you NAMESERVERS -> update them at your domain REGISTRAR
    4. once active, you manage all DNS in Cloudflare
  THE PROXY TOGGLE (orange cloud — Cloudflare-specific & important):
    · PROXIED (orange) = traffic goes THROUGH Cloudflare (CDN, caching, security all apply)
    · DNS-ONLY (grey) = Cloudflare just answers DNS; traffic goes straight to origin
    -> proxy your web traffic to get Cloudflare's benefits; some records (mail) stay DNS-only
  PROPAGATION: DNS changes can take time to spread globally (minutes to hours)
KEY: A/CNAME/MX/TXT records point things; add a site via nameservers; the ORANGE CLOUD = proxied.

The common record types: A (name → IPv4), AAAA (→ IPv6), CNAME (name → another name), MX (mail servers), TXT (verification, email auth like SPF/DKIM), and NS (nameservers). Adding a site: add your domain, Cloudflare scans your existing records, gives you nameservers to set at your registrar, and once active you manage all DNS in Cloudflare. The proxy toggle (the orange cloud) is Cloudflare-specific and important: proxied (orange) means traffic goes through Cloudflare so CDN, caching, and security all apply; DNS-only (grey) means Cloudflare just answers DNS and traffic goes straight to your origin — so you proxy your web traffic to get the benefits, while some records (like mail) stay DNS-only. And propagation means DNS changes take time to spread. Mastering records, adding a site via nameservers, and the orange-cloud proxy concept is the DNS foundation.

2.3 DNS as the Foundation

DNS deserves emphasis because it's the foundation everything else depends on, and getting it right (or wrong) has outsized impact. Almost every Cloudflare capability flows from DNS being configured correctly: the proxy (orange cloud) is what routes traffic through Cloudflare so caching and security can work; pointing your domain to the right place is what makes your site load at all; email records (MX, TXT) keep your mail working; and verification records (TXT) prove ownership for various services. DNS is also where many real-world problems live — a misconfigured record, a wrong proxy setting, or DNS that hasn't propagated is behind a large share of 'the site is broken' incidents — so being confident with DNS makes you genuinely useful (DNS troubleshooting is a valued skill). Cloudflare's DNS is fast, free, and easy to manage, making it a great place to learn. The key understandings to lock in: what each record type does, how to add and manage records, how adding a site (via nameservers) works, the critical proxy toggle, and that changes propagate over time. With a domain on Cloudflare and DNS understood, you have the foundation for everything else — caching, security, rules, and the developer platform all build on correctly-configured DNS. A domain properly set up on Cloudflare, with sensible records and the right proxy settings, demonstrates the foundational skill the rest of Cloudflare rests on.

CHECKPOINT

Get a domain onto Cloudflare and manage its DNS: add a site (set the nameservers at your registrar), and configure DNS records — an A or CNAME for your site (proxied, orange cloud), and understand MX/TXT for mail/verification. Grasp the proxy toggle and propagation. DNS is the foundation everything on Cloudflare (and the web) depends on — a correctly-configured domain is the base for caching, security, and the developer platform.
