7.1 Communication and Coordination Are the Job

A huge part of any job — especially remote work — is communicating and coordinating, and Google's tools for this (Gmail for email, Google Calendar for scheduling, Google Meet for video meetings) are where much of that happens for the millions of teams on Workspace. Doing this well and efficiently is a real, valuable skill: managing email so it doesn't manage you, keeping an organised calendar, communicating professionally, and running and joining meetings effectively. For remote work particularly, these tools are the workplace — they're how you stay connected, coordinate across distance, and get work done together. Strong skills here make you organised, responsive, and a good collaborator (qualities employers prize), while poor ones (chaotic inbox, missed messages, disorganised calendar) hold you and your team back. And Gmail is one of the most widely used email systems in the world, so fluency is broadly useful. Understanding how to use Gmail, Calendar, and Meet efficiently and professionally is a practical competence that underpins effectiveness in almost any modern role, and it's especially important for the remote work this school focuses on.

7.2 Gmail, Calendar, and Meet (Practical)

GMAIL, CALENDAR & MEET
  GMAIL (manage it, don't drown in it):
    · ORGANISE: LABELS (Gmail's flexible 'folders' — a message can have several) ·
      FILTERS (auto-label/archive/sort incoming mail) · stars/important · powerful SEARCH
    · INBOX management: archive (not delete) · a system (process to a clean inbox) ·
      categories/tabs · snooze · keyboard shortcuts (turn them on — big speed boost)
    · write PROFESSIONAL email: clear subject, concise, polite, clear ask/next step · signature
    · etiquette: reply-all wisely · CC/BCC correctly · timely responses
  GOOGLE CALENDAR:
    · create events/meetings · invite guests (auto-adds a Meet link) · 'Find a time' (availability) ·
      reminders · recurring events · multiple/shared calendars · block focus time · working hours
  GOOGLE MEET (video meetings):
    · start/join (from Calendar or directly) · share screen · record · chat · captions
    · meeting etiquette (prepared, present, mute when not speaking)
  INTEGRATION: it all links — email -> calendar event -> Meet, Drive attachments, etc.
KEY: manage Gmail with labels/filters + a system, keep an organised Calendar, run/join Meet well
  — professional, efficient communication.

Gmail — manage it, don't drown: organise with labels (Gmail's flexible 'folders' — a message can have several) and filters (auto-labelling/archiving/sorting incoming mail), plus stars and powerful search; run your inbox with a system (archive rather than delete, process to a clean inbox, use snooze, and turn on keyboard shortcuts for a big speed boost); write professional email (clear subject, concise, polite, clear ask); and follow etiquette. Google Calendar: create events/meetings, invite guests (which auto-adds a Meet link), use 'Find a time' for availability, set reminders and recurring events, manage shared calendars, and block focus time. Google Meet: start/join (from Calendar or directly), share screen, record, chat, captions, with good meeting etiquette. And it all integrates (email → calendar event → Meet, Drive attachments). Manage Gmail with labels/filters and a system, keep an organised Calendar, and run/join Meet well.

7.3 Being an Effective Remote Collaborator

Mastering these tools is about being an effective, professional remote collaborator — organised, responsive, easy to work with — which is what makes remote work function and what employers value. Gmail management is foundational: a system (labels, filters, a way of processing your inbox to keep it under control) plus clear, professional, actionable emails makes you reliable and easy to work with (a chaotic emailer is a liability) — and Gmail's labels/filters/search and keyboard shortcuts make this very achievable. Calendar discipline keeps you organised and coordinated — managing your time, meetings, and availability so you're dependable and protect focused time — and shared calendars are how remote teams coordinate. Meet skills matter enormously for remote work — joining and running video meetings well (prepared, present, good etiquette, smooth screen-sharing) is how distributed teams actually meet and collaborate. The tight integration (email to calendar to Meet, all linked, all in the cloud) makes coordinating smooth once you're fluent. Together, these make you the organised, communicative, collaborative professional that remote teams depend on — a genuinely valuable, if underrated, set of skills, and directly relevant since so many remote-first companies run on exactly these Google tools. Demonstrating efficient, professional communication and coordination — a managed Gmail inbox, an organised Calendar, smooth Meet use — shows the remote-work effectiveness that underpins success in modern roles.

CHECKPOINT

Build efficient communication habits: set up a Gmail system (labels, filters, a processing routine, keyboard shortcuts) and write clear professional emails, keep an organised Calendar (scheduling, 'find a time', focus blocks, shared calendars), and run/join Meet well (screen share, etiquette). Being an organised, responsive, professional remote collaborator — on the very tools remote-first teams use — is a prized skill that underpins modern work.
